## Rule 1: Always review AI output

**AI is a powerful assistant, but you're the expert.**

### Why this matters

AI can produce impressive results quickly — but it can also make mistakes, invent facts (hallucinations), or miss important nuances.

Your job isn't just to use AI. It's to ensure the final result is accurate, appropriate and professionally sound.

### What this means in practice

AI should help you work better, not think less.

**✅ Good uses of AI (enhancing your work):**

Check facts — Verify any statistics, dates, names or claims

Read critically — Does it actually answer the question? Is the logic sound?

Match your voice — Does it sound like you/Danish Agro, or is it generic?

Consider context — Is it appropriate for the audience and situation?

Ask yourself — "Would I be comfortable signing my name to this?"

**❌ Bad uses of AI (replacing your thinking):**

Copy-pasting AI output without reading it carefully

Accepting facts and figures without verification

Using generic AI language instead of your own voice

Skipping the review because "AI is usually right"

Trusting AI output more than your own judgment

*The test: If someone asks "Why did you choose this approach?" can you explain it in your own words?*

### Real example

**Situation:** You ask ChatGPT to write a social media post about Danish Agro's sustainability initiatives.

**What AI gives you:**

> "Danish Agro has reduced CO2 emissions by 40% since 2020 and is carbon neutral across all operations."

**The problem:**

Those specific numbers and claims need verification. If they're wrong, it damages credibility.

**What you should do:**

- Check internal data — are those numbers accurate?
- Verify the timeline — is "since 2020" correct?
- Confirm the claim — are we actually carbon neutral?
- Adjust the text based on verified facts

**Better output after review:**

> "Danish Agro is committed to reducing our environmental impact. We're making progress on emissions across our operations — learn more about our sustainability journey [link]."

### Quick checklist

Use this every time before sharing AI-generated content:

- [ ] I've verified all facts and numbers
- [ ] The tone matches our brand and the situation
- [ ] There are no obvious errors or inconsistencies
- [ ] I understand what it says (not just copy-pasted)
- [ ] I would be comfortable defending this content

### When you're not sure

If something feels off but you can't pinpoint why:

- **Ask a colleague to review it** "Does this sound like me or like AI?"
- **Use the Quality Checklist** Review against our quality standards
- **Start over with a different approach** Sometimes a fresh prompt works better
- **Contact the AI Playbook team** We're here to help guide you

### When you're not sure

If you're worried you're relying too much on AI:

**Take a break**

Step away and come back with fresh eyes

**Ask a colleague**

"Does this sound like me or like AI?"

**Rewrite key sections**

In your own voice

**Add specific examples**

Only you would know

**Start with your thinking**

Write your own ideas first, then use AI to enhance

## Why we have this rule

71% of employees worry most about the quality of AI output — not data security or compliance.

You care about doing good work. This rule helps you maintain your professional standards while benefiting from AI's speed and creativity.

**Remember:** Using AI doesn't mean accepting everything it gives you. It means being a smart editor and quality controller.
