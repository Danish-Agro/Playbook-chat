## Mistakes happen — here's what to do

You realised you shared something you shouldn't have with an AI tool. Maybe it was customer data, internal numbers, or personal information. It happens. What matters now is how you respond.

---

**Don't panic.** The most important thing is that you react quickly and openly.

**Step 1: Stop using the tool**

Close the conversation and don't share anything more.

**Step 2: Contact IT or your manager**

Explain what you shared and in what context. The sooner, the better.

**Step 3: Document**

Write down briefly:

- Which tool did you use?
- What did you share?
- When did it happen?

**Step 4: Learn from it**

We use incidents as learning – not as punishment. Your openness helps all of us become better.

## Why reporting matters

When you report an incident, you help us:

- Assess if any action is needed (often it isn't)
- Prevent similar mistakes across the organisation
- Improve our guidelines and training

Keeping it to yourself doesn't make it go away — it just means we can't help.

---

## What happens when you report

1. **IT Security assesses the risk** — What was shared? How sensitive? What's the potential impact?
2. **They advise on next steps** — Often no action is needed. Sometimes we notify affected parties.
3. **We close the loop** — You'll hear back on what happened and what we learned.

No blame. No punishment. Just a professional response to a professional challenge.
