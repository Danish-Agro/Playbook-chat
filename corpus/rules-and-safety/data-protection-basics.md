## Tools and their data security

Not all AI tools handle data the same way. Here are the most commonly used:

### Microsoft Copilot (Paid version with M365)

✅ **Recommended for internal use**

- Data is not shared with OpenAI
- Follows Danish Agro's M365 security policy
- GDPR-compliant
- Can be used for internal documentation (still with caution)

### ChatGPT (Free version)

⚠️ **Use with caution**

- Data can be used to train the model
- No guarantee for data security
- Only for open, non-sensitive tasks
- Never for customer data or internal numbers

### ChatGPT Plus / Team / Enterprise

🟡 **Better, but not approved**

- Data is not used for training
- Better privacy handling
- But still not officially approved at Danish Agro
- Use only for non-sensitive content

### Claude, Gemini, and others

⚠️ **Same level as ChatGPT Free**

- Not approved
- Assume data can be stored
- Use only for open content

## Anonymization: How to do it

If you want to use AI to work with internal data, you can often do so by anonymizing first.

### Method 1: Replace names and numbers

**Before:** "Peter Jensen sold 47,000 kg of feed to Farm Ltd. for a value of DKK 342,000."

**After:** "A salesperson sold X kg of feed to a customer for a value of Y DKK."

### Method 2: Use only the structure

**Before:** "Customer email from Jens Larsen complaining about late delivery to CVR 12345678"

**After:** "A customer sent an email complaining about delivery. How should I respond professionally?"

### Method 3: Generalize the scenario

**Before:** "Our Q3 sales in Germany fell by 12%, and we lost the contract with Müller GmbH"

**After:** "If a company experiences declining sales in a market, what strategic measures can be considered?"

# Data Protection Basics

## What you need to know about data security

You're probably already using AI in your work – and that's completely fine. But when we work with AI tools, we're sending data outside Danish Agro's systems. That's why it's important you know what you can share – and what you can't.

This isn't about stopping you. It's about helping you use AI safely, so you can leverage the technology without putting the company or our customers in a vulnerable position.

---

## The basic rule: Think before you share

When you use an AI tool (ChatGPT, Copilot, or others), always assume that:

- Data can be stored by the tool
- Data can be used to train future models
- Data can be visible to others (depending on settings)
- You don't have full control over where it ends up

**Therefore:** Never share anything you wouldn't put on a public drive.

---

## ✅ What you can share

### General knowledge and open data

- Publicly available information
- Anonymized datasets
- General industry facts
- Open market analyses
- Published financial reports (after publication)

### Your own texts for improvement

- Texts you've written yourself (when they don't contain sensitive data)
- Drafts for blog posts, press releases, or campaign texts
- Generic product descriptions
- Internal communication templates (without names, numbers, or details)

### Examples of everyday use

✅ "Rewrite this product description to make it more engaging"
✅ "Create a list of 10 headlines for a blog post about sustainability in agriculture"
✅ "Explain the difference between GDP and GNP"
✅ "Help me structure meeting minutes"

---

## ❌ What you CANNOT share

### Personal data (GDPR)

- Names of employees or customers
- Email addresses
- Phone numbers
- Social security numbers
- Addresses
- Employment information
- Salary data
- Health information

### Customer data

- Customer contracts
- Customer revenue
- Customer-specific prices or discounts
- Delivery agreements
- Internal customer assessments

### Internal business data

- Unpublished financial reports
- Strategic plans
- M&A preparations
- Pricing models
- Unreleased product launches
- Sales budgets
- Internal financial analyses

### Competitively sensitive data

- Market analyses revealing our strategy
- Competitor assessments with internal notes
- Supplier agreements with prices
- Discount structures

### Technical security data

- API keys
- Passwords
- System architectures
- Security procedures

---

## 🟡 Gray areas: Ask if you're in doubt

Some situations aren't clear-cut. If you're in doubt, stop and ask first.

### Examples of gray areas

- Summarizing an internal report (can it be done without sensitive details?)
- Rewriting a customer quote (does it contain pricing information?)
- Brainstorming about a new product strategy (is it competitively sensitive?)
- Analyzing internal sales figures (can it be sufficiently anonymized?)

**Solution:** Ask your manager, IT department, or write to the AI team. It's better to ask once too many than to share something that shouldn't be shared.

## Practical examples from everyday work

### ✅ Example 1: Product description

**Task:** I need to write a product description for our website about roughage.

**What I share with AI:** "Write an engaging product description for roughage for dairy cows. Focus on high energy, good digestibility, and local production."

**Why it's okay:** Generic product info without prices, customers, or internal strategies.

---

### ❌ Example 2: Customer email (wrong)

**Task:** I need to respond to a customer complaint.

**What I should NOT share:** "Jens Nielsen from Farm Ltd. wrote to us on December 3rd complaining that delivery 10347 was missing 200 kg of feed for CVR 87654321."

**Why it's wrong:** Contains customer name, CVR, order number, and sensitive details.

### ✅ Example 2: Customer email (correct)

**What I share instead:** "A customer has written a complaint about a missing delivery. How do I write a professional response that acknowledges the problem and suggests a solution?"

**Why it's okay:** Anonymized, no personal data, generic scenario.

---

### 🟡 Example 3: Internal report (gray area)

**Task:** I need to summarize our Q4 sales figures.

**Uncertainty:** Is it okay to use AI to summarize an internal sales report?

**Solution:**

- If the report is only used internally and contains competitively sensitive numbers: NO
- If you can remove all specific numbers and only ask for structuring: MAYBE
- If you're in doubt: ASK
