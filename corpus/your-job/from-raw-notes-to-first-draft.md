# From Raw Notes to First Draft

How to turn scattered product facts and rough notes into sharp first drafts for newsletters, social posts, product copy, and sales material.

---

## The use case

You have product facts, bullet points, and scattered notes. You need a polished first draft adapted to a specific audience, channel, and purpose. AI can handle the structure and drafting work so you can focus on the commercial angle, quality, and final judgment.

> The goal is to remove 60-80% of the groundwork, not to remove the person. AI does the draft. You make the final call.

---

## How it works

Give the AI three things and it will give you a usable first draft:

1. Raw input: paste in your product facts, notes, or bullet points directly. Do not describe them, just paste them.
2. Audience: who is this for? A customer, a retailer, a new employee?
3. Format and purpose: newsletter intro, LinkedIn post, product description, sales email?

---

## Prompt structure that works

Here are my raw notes: [paste notes]. Turn this into a [newsletter intro / LinkedIn post / product description / sales email] for [audience]. Tone: [direct / warm / commercial / informative]. Keep it under [word count]. I will edit the final version myself.

---

## Examples by format

Newsletter intro: "Here are my notes on our new feed product launch. Turn this into a 100-word newsletter intro for Danish livestock farmers. Tone: direct and informative."

LinkedIn post: "Here are the key facts about our sustainability initiative. Turn this into a LinkedIn post for a professional audience. Keep it under 150 words and end with a question."

Product description: "Here are the technical specs and benefits of this product. Write a product description for our website aimed at farmers with mid-size operations. Focus on practical benefits, not features."

Sales email: "Here are my notes from the customer meeting. Draft a follow-up sales email based on what we discussed. Tone: warm and confident."

---

## Tips for better results

- Paste raw input directly rather than describing it. Direct input consistently produces better output.
- If you work with the same product categories or audiences regularly, save a prompt template with your tone of voice and audience description. It saves time across all future drafts.
- AI is good at structure and flow. It does not know your commercial angle or strategic priorities. That is your job.
- Always review and edit the output. The draft is a starting point, not a finished product.
- For content involving customer data or internal product information, use Copilot rather than external tools.
