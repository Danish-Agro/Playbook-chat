# What is AI?

## Get the basics – without the jargon

You don't need a technical background to understand AI. This section gives you a clear, practical introduction to what generative AI actually is, how it works, and what it can (and can't) do.

Whether you're brand new to AI or just want to make sure you've got the fundamentals right, start here.

## More than just ChatGPT

When people talk about AI at work, they usually mean ChatGPT or similar tools. But generative AI is broader than that. Today's AI tools can create and work with:

**Text**

Write, analyse, translate, and summarise. Tools like ChatGPT, Microsoft Copilot, Claude, and Gemini are the most common AI tools in the workplace.

**Images**

Generate images from descriptions, edit photos, remove backgrounds, and create variations. Tools like DALL-E, Midjourney, and Adobe Firefly.

**Video**

Generate video clips, edit footage, add effects, and create animations. Tools like Runway, Pika, and Sora.

**Audio**

Generate voiceovers, clone voices, create music, and transcribe speech. Tools like ElevenLabs, Murf, and Suno.

Most of this Playbook focuses on text-based AI tools – they're what 90% of us use daily. But the principles of responsible use apply across all types.

## Why this matters

AI is already part of how we work at Danish Agro. 96% of employees use AI tools regularly – but that doesn't mean everyone feels confident about what's actually happening when they use these tools.

Understanding the basics helps you:

- Use AI more effectively across different types of tasks
- Spot mistakes and limitations in AI output – whether it's text, images, or something else
- Make informed decisions about when AI is helpful – and when it's not
- Feel confident explaining your AI use to colleagues or managers

You don't need to become an expert. But knowing the fundamentals makes you a smarter, more critical user.

## What you'll learn

This section covers three topics that give you a solid foundation:

**Simple explanation**

What generative AI actually is, in plain terms. We'll explain what it means for AI to "generate" content – whether that's text, images, video, or audio. You'll learn how these tools work at a basic level and what you can use them for in your daily work.

**How it works**

A look under the hood – without the technical complexity. We'll explain concepts like training data, models, and prompts in a way that makes sense. Understanding the basics helps you understand why AI sometimes gets things wrong.

**Common misconceptions**

There's a lot of confusion about what AI can and can't do. This page separates myths from reality: Does AI understand what it's saying? Can it access the internet? Are AI-generated images "real"? Knowing the answers helps you use AI with the right expectations.

## Before you dive in

A quick reminder: AI is a tool, not a replacement for your skills and judgment. The goal of this section isn't to turn you into an AI specialist – it's to give you just enough understanding to use AI confidently, critically, and effectively in your work.

If you're already comfortable with the basics, feel free to skip ahead to Rules and Safety or Quality Assurance. But if you've ever wondered "how does this thing actually work?" – you're in the right place.
